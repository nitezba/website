The best way I've thought of to translate that feeling to reading a textbook is a more organized way of

  1. Read an entire chapter (or big chunk) on Monday. The aim here isn't to create comprehension right away, but rather to leave behind a breadcrumb trail of questions to fill in throughout the week, and answer as a test at the end of the week.
  2. On the same day, make a detailed plan of attack for the rest of the week with concrete benchmarks.
  3. Read according to that plan, preferably with the read + recall method.
  4. Test!

The idea behind this is to try and incorporate more free recall into the studying, which is hard but is also part of the fun I've rediscovered in SDL. The only "problem" with free recall is the that in order to recall something you have to be presented with it first, hence the Monday speedrun.

The plan for this week then is to make that work on part of, if not all of Chapter 2 of Munkres. Given my track record, the entire chapter is ambitious but I'll set a solid goal as I read on Monday.
