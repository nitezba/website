Not hard long, moreso tedious long, and that combined with me thinking too hard sometimes and nitpicking over proof details (happened multiple times this week), I didn't push far.

I do think I came up with a useful system though:

  1. Preread - read everything in the chapter but don't worry at all about retention; instead just take notes in the form of questions. Ideally, these will serve as an evaluation/feedback method once the material isn't in immediate memory but I didn't test that this week.
  2. Free recall reading - the kind of reading I was trying to employ before: read a page or two (or until stopping point) close book and take notes from memory, as though you were trying to teach the material immediately.
  3. Switch - to (assigned) problems from previous chapters, preferably away from technology and just on a printed sheet of paper.
  4. wait for it...
  5. Self-test - at the end of study sesh, review questionss written in that first preread.

Neato! I'm feeling a little down on the fact that I'm actually so short on progress in the book but these takeaways are at least comfort that I haven't entirely wasted my time. I definitely have wasted some of it though, and again, some days this week were proof to myself that I am capable of being as intense about this as I really want to be; that I won't let short-term immediate rewards get in the way.
